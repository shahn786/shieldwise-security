### Section 1: Verify California BSIS Licensing (200 words)
**Why it matters:** All security guards in California must be licensed by the Bureau of Security and Investigative Services (BSIS).

**What to check:**
- Guard Card (required for all security personnel)
- Firearms Permit (for armed guards)
- Company license number
- Verify at: https://www.bsis.ca.gov

**Red flag:** Any company that can't immediately provide their BSIS license number.

### Section 2: Insurance & Bonding (150 words)
**Essential coverage:**
- General liability insurance ($1-2M minimum)
- Workers' compensation
- Professional liability insurance

**Why it matters:** If a security guard is injured or causes damage, proper insurance protects YOU from liability.

**What to ask:** "Can you provide proof of current insurance certificates?"

### Section 3: Experience in Your Industry (200 words)

**Construction sites:** Theft prevention, access control

**Corporate:** Executive protection, access control

**What to ask:** "How many clients do you serve in [my industry]? Can I speak with references?"

- Unarmed guards: $25-$40/hour

**Factors affecting price:**
- Location (urban vs rural)

**Red flag:** Prices significantly below market rate (likely unlicensed or undertrained guards)

## Template 2: "Armed vs Unarmed Security Guards: Which Does Your California Business Need?"

### Introduction (100 words)
One of the most important decisions when hiring security is whether you need armed or unarmed guards. The wrong choice can either waste money on unnecessary firepower or leave your business vulnerable. This guide explains the key differences, legal requirements in California, and which option is best for different business types.

### Armed Security Guards: Overview (200 words)
**What they are:**
- Licensed to carry firearms on duty
- Additional BSIS firearms permit required
- Extensive weapons training and certification
- Annual recertification mandatory

**When to use armed security:**
- High-crime areas
- Banks and financial institutions
- Jewelry stores
- Cannabis dispensaries
- Properties with valuable inventory
- Executive protection
- Cash-heavy businesses
- Government facilities
- High-risk construction sites

**California requirements:**
- Guard Card + Firearms Permit
- 14-hour Firearms Training Course
- Live-fire qualification
- Annual requalification
- Background check (more stringent)

**Costs:** $35-$55/hour in California

### Unarmed Security Guards: Overview (200 words)
**What they are:**
- Professional security without firearms
- Focus on observation, reporting, deterrence
- Customer service oriented
- De-escalation trained

**When to use unarmed security:**
- Retail stores and shopping malls
- Office buildings
- Apartment complexes
- Hotels and hospitality
- Schools and universities
- Hospitals and healthcare facilities
- Event security
- Residential communities
- Low to moderate risk environments

**California requirements:**
- Guard Card (16-hour training)
- Background check
- Professional appearance
- Communication skills

**Costs:** $25-$40/hour in California

### Legal Considerations in California (200 words)

**Important California laws:**

1. **Use of Force:** Armed guards can only use firearms when reasonable person would believe life is threatened

2. **Liability:** Business owners are liable for improper use of force by armed guards

3. **Training Requirements:** Must verify guard has current firearms permit

4. **Insurance:** Armed guard services require higher liability coverage

5. **Restrictions:** Some California cities have additional regulations on armed security

**Questions to ask your security company:**
